Our Story

Cuarteto Metanoia was founded in 2020 by four passionate musicians who shared a vision: to create a string quartet that would bring the highest level of artistry to chamber music performance in Colombia and beyond.

The name 'Metanoia' comes from the Greek word meaning 'transformation' or 'change of heart.' This reflects our belief that music has the power to transform both performers and listeners, creating profound connections through shared artistic experiences.

Since our founding, we have performed at prestigious venues across Colombia, collaborated with renowned international artists, and built a reputation for musical excellence and innovative programming that respects tradition while embracing contemporary voices.

Our Mission

To inspire audiences through exceptional performances of classical and contemporary chamber music, while fostering a deeper appreciation for the string quartet tradition.

Artistic Excellence

We are committed to the highest standards of musical interpretation and technical precision.

Innovation & Tradition

We honor the great works of the past while championing new voices in chamber music.

Community Engagement

We believe in making classical music accessible and relevant to diverse audiences.

Collaboration

We seek opportunities to work with other artists and organizations to expand our musical horizons.

Meet Our Musicians

Four Exceptional Artists, One Unified Voice

Laura Martínez

First Violin

Laura holds a Master's degree from the Juilliard School and has performed as a soloist with major orchestras across the Americas. Her passionate and nuanced playing brings depth to every performance.

Education

  • Juilliard School (MM)
  • Conservatorio Nacional (BM)

Notable Achievements

  • Winner, International Violin Competition of Indianapolis
  • Former Concertmaster, Orquesta Filarmónica de Bogotá

Diego Ramírez

Second Violin

Diego's versatile musicianship and collaborative spirit make him an essential voice in the quartet. He studied at the Royal Academy of Music in London and has extensive experience in both classical and contemporary repertoire.

Education

  • Royal Academy of Music (MM)
  • Universidad Nacional (BM)

Notable Achievements

  • Prizewinner, ARD International Music Competition
  • Former member, Lucerne Festival Orchestra

Camila Torres

Viola

Camila's rich, warm tone and profound musical insight anchor the quartet's sound. A graduate of the Curtis Institute, she has performed chamber music with some of the world's leading musicians.

Education

  • Curtis Institute of Music (MM)
  • Conservatorio Javeriano (BM)

Notable Achievements

  • Winner, Primrose International Viola Competition
  • Faculty, Festival Internacional de Música de Cartagena

Santiago Vargas

Cello

Santiago's powerful yet sensitive playing provides the foundation for the quartet's interpretations. He studied with legendary cellists in Europe and brings a wealth of experience from both solo and chamber music performances.

Education

  • Hochschule für Musik Hanns Eisler (MM)
  • Conservatorio Nacional (BM)

Notable Achievements

  • Finalist, International Tchaikovsky Competition
  • Principal Cello, Colombian National Symphony Orchestra
